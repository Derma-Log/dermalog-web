Important notice

Derma.Log is a private dermatology logging and observation tool. It helps users document, organize, review, export, and discuss skin observations over time.

Derma.Log does not diagnose disease, rule out disease, replace medical professionals, provide emergency care, or make clinical treatment decisions.

Not medical advice

Information shown in Derma.Log is for personal documentation and informational organization only. Derma.Log does not provide medical advice.

Users should not use Derma.Log as a substitute for medical advice, diagnosis, examination, treatment, or follow-up from a qualified medical professional.

No diagnosis or cancer clearance

Derma.Log does not diagnose skin cancer, melanoma, infection, inflammatory disease, autoimmune disease, or any other medical condition. Derma.Log does not rule out cancer or other serious conditions.

A spot, rash, mole, lesion, wound, discoloration, or skin change may still require medical assessment even if Derma.Log presents it calmly, labels it as stable, or shows no obvious change.

Risk, analysis, and visualization surfaces

Derma.Log may present structured summaries, visualizations, risk-related labels, development patterns, or timeline information. These surfaces are intended to help users organize observations and communicate more clearly.

They are not clinical conclusions and do not replace professional examination, dermoscopy, biopsy, laboratory testing, imaging, diagnosis, or treatment planning.

Images and reminders

Derma.Log allows users to store and compare skin images over time. Photo quality, lighting, angle, distance, focus, skin tone, camera behavior, compression, and user input can affect how images appear.

Reminders are organizational tools. A reminder does not determine whether medical care is needed, and missing or disabling a reminder is not medical guidance.

Exports and reports

Derma.Log exports may help users organize and share their history with a medical professional. Exports do not create a diagnosis.

Reports are structured summaries for communication and review. They are not medical records created by a clinician unless a clinician separately reviews and adopts them. Users should review exports before sharing.

Urgent care

Derma.Log is not an emergency service. Users should seek urgent medical help or contact local emergency services if they experience symptoms that may require immediate care.

Users should not wait for Derma.Log reminders, exports, analysis, or app feedback before seeking urgent care.
